Research

Research Interests

My research focuses on cryptographic approaches to enhancing trust and privacy in emerging technologies through advanced cryptographic primitives and privacy-preserving protocols with provable security guarantees.

  • Identity-based signatures, proxy signatures, multi-proxy signatures, multi-signatures, and adaptor signatures
  • Online/offline signatures and signcryption for mobile and multi-device systems
  • Provable security and cryptographic security models
  • Privacy-preserving horizontal and vertical federated learning
  • Blockchain payment channels, payment-channel hubs, and scalable digital payments
  • CBDC privacy, fine-grained auditability, and digital-asset transaction tracing
  • Privacy-preserving transactions among autonomous AI agents

Doctoral Research

My dissertation studies practical and provably secure online/offline cryptographic protocols for mobile applications. The first part establishes the importance of separating proxy signing keys from long-term private keys and develops an identity-based multi-proxy signature scheme for Internet-of-Drones environments.

The second part identifies a memory-corruption threat between offline and online phases in multi-device environments and proposes identity-based on/offline signcryption schemes designed to remain secure against such exposure.
